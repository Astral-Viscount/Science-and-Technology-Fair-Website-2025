Science vs Technology

Both science and technology are important at the fair. Understanding the difference helps you plan a project that meets judging expectations.

Scientific investigations

  • Question-driven: Pose a testable question and hypothesis.
  • Method: Collect data through experiments and observations.
  • Analysis: Use statistics or clear comparisons to draw conclusions.

Technology & design projects

  • Problem-driven: Identify a real-world problem and design a solution.
  • Build & test: Prototype, iterate, and evaluate performance.
  • Evidence: Provide tests or demonstrations that show your solution works.
