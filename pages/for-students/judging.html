Judging

Judges evaluate entries using a rubric that typically covers:

  • Clarity of the question or problem
  • Appropriateness and rigour of the method
  • Quality of data and analysis
  • Originality and significance
  • Presentation and communication skills

Prepare for the interview

You will speak to judges for a short time. Practice these points:

  1. Explain your question in one sentence.
  2. Summarise your method and why it is fair/reliable.
  3. Highlight the key result and what it means.
  4. Mention limitations and next steps.

Score sheets & feedback

Judges provide scores and short feedback. Use feedback to improve future work. See the Judging Criteria (PDF) for sample scores and guidance.
