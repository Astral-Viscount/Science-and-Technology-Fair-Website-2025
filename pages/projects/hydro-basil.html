Optimising Hydroponic Basil

By Charlotte Ng â€” Year 10 â€¢ Category: Biology, Agriculture

Aim

To determine which nutrient mix produces the best basil growth (biomass and flavour) in a compact, home hydroponic setup.

Method

  1. Three nutrient mixes prepared: A (standard), B (higher nitrogen), C (organic compost tea).
  2. Identical seedlings planted in NFT channels with controlled light and pH.
  3. Measurements every 3 days: height, leaf count, and biomass at harvest (21 days).
  4. Sensory test (small panel) for flavour preference.

Results

  • Mix B (higher nitrogen) produced the greatest biomass but slightly reduced flavour intensity.
  • Mix C (compost tea) yielded robust flavour scores but more variability in growth.
  • pH stability correlated strongly with consistent growth.

Conclusions

For home growers seeking maximum yield, slightly higher nitrogen works well; for those prioritising flavour, organic mixes are promising. Ensuring stable pH is essential.
