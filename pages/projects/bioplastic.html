Bio-plastic from Kelp

By Rangi Taiaroa — Year 12 • Category: Environment, Chemistry

Summary

This project investigates whether polysaccharides extracted from locally-harvested kelp can be processed into a biodegradable plastic substitute. It evaluates strength, flexibility, and decomposition time under composting conditions.

Method

  1. Collected small quantities of kelp from permitted local sites.
  2. Extracted polysaccharides using hot water and filtration.
  3. Mixed extracts with glycerol and heated to form a uniform solution.
  4. Poured into trays and allowed to dry into films.
  5. Tested mechanical properties (tensile strength) and compostability.

Results

  • Films showed reasonable flexibility with addition of glycerol (plasticiser).
  • Tensile strength was lower than commercial plastics but sufficient for lightweight packaging prototypes.
  • Composting tests showed 65–80% breakdown within 12 weeks depending on formulation.

Conclusions & Next steps

Kelp-derived bioplastics appear promising for low-strength applications. Next steps include optimizing extraction yield, reducing cost, and testing for food-contact safety.
